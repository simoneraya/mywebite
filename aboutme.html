Welcome to my blog! I am a Toronto-born, London-based student pursuing a dual MA/MSc degree in International History at Columbia University and the LSE. I graduated from McGill University in 2023, where I studied history, political science, and South Asian studies. This interdisciplinary background sparked my thematic interest in democracy development in differing contexts, particularly in the intersection of foreign policy and democracy. I was fascinated by the comparative histories of the United States and South Asia and was eager to continue my studies in a program that emphasizes international dynamics and relations.

As a critical thinker, I am deeply passionate about exploring historical events and understanding their profound impact on our modern world. I find fulfilment in investigating historical narratives, deconstructing accepted viewpoints, and challenging conventional wisdom to develop a more nuanced and informed perspective. I try to practice a fluidity between critical thinking and creative expression - I’m not just interested in studying the world, but in creating within it - whether through writing, advocacy, or even website design.

Beyond intellectual interests, I enjoy reading, traveling, movies, and engaging in thought-provoking discussions. I am driven by a desire to better understand global processes and historical cycles. This website serves as a portfolio of my research interests, professional experiences, and some of my thoughts published on my blog.
